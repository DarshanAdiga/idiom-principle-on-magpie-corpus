 # Acadia Parish sheriff: 14-year-old Church Point boy dies after shooting
 Ashley White
 Lafayette Daily Advertiser
 A 14-year-old boy is dead and one person was arrested after a shooting in Church Point.
 The teen was shot Tuesday night near South McMillan and East Ebey streets. His identity has not been released.
 Nicholas Ardoin, 18, is accused of firing multiple shots at two victims who were walking by, hitting the 14-year-old, according to a Facebook post from Acadia Parish Sheriff K.P. Gibson. A juvenile was questioned in connection with the shooting.
 Ardoin, of Church Point, was arrested and charged with second-degree murder, attempted second-degree murder and aggravated criminal damage to property.
 Ardoin was booked into the Acadia Parish Jail and his bond was set at $510,000.
 Anyone with information about the shooting should call detectives at 337-788-8721 or Crime Stoppers of Acadia Parish at 337-789-8477.
