Browns veteran offensive lineman Joel Bitonio might be the man in the middle during the early part of the season, but even with center JC Tretter sidelined, he won't be playing that position.
   
 Bitonio, a two-time Pro Bowl left guard, finds himself mentoring two rookies -- center Nick Harris and left tackle Jedrick Wills.
   
 Tretter had knee surgery Thursday. He has a good chance to return in time for the Browns' Sept. 13 season opener against the Baltimore Ravens, though that remains uncertain, a league source said.
   
 "He's seen a lot of defenses and a lot of fronts. He knows how to make those calls and, you know, bring that experience," Bitonio said of Tretter. "You bring that rookie center in like Nick, he has talent, he can move and do that well, but he just hasn't been exposed to as many of the looks that we're going to see. So it's up to me and [right guard] Wyatt [Teller] and [right tackle] Jack Conklin and those guys and [quarterback] Baker [Mayfield] to help him along as much as possible."
   
 Harris, a 6-foot-1, 302-pound fifth-round draft choice, currently fills Tretter's role with the first-team offense, and, barring injury, that is unlikely to change.
   
 "So far, so good. All of these young players, it is never perfect, and they are constantly learning." Browns coach Kevin Stefanski said via Zoom after practice Sunday. "It is going to be a tough go early for these guys just as they are taking in all of the information and then the physicality picks up. I would not single Nick out from anybody else. Just know that those guys, there are growing pains to this. We totally understand that. It is our job to push them and make sure he is ready to roll if he is the guy."
   
 Bitonio said he considers it a good sign Tretter has been at team headquarters since having surgery, but the Browns are preparing for the worst-case scenario should his recovery be delayed. And Bitonio will be the linchpin should he be forced into the role of tutor.
   
 Wills' preparation would certainly be helped if defensive end Myles Garrett were not currently out with a hamstring injury.
   
 "Jed's going to have a big test," Bitonio said. "Right now, he still has guys like [defensive end Olivier Vernon] out there, who's a great player in his own right, so he's still getting challenged. It's good because practice is going to be faster than the game. It's already faster than the game. Then you get Myles, too, and then it's really going to step up. And once we get to the games, hopefully it slows down for him a little bit."
   
 Stefanski understands what having Garrett in there can do for Wills, but said he has confidence Vernon can challenge the rookie from the University of Alabama, who is transitioning from right to left tackle.
   
 "I think that is great when you get good against good. I think that will only help Jedrick in his development," he said. "I talked to Olivier today, and we're pushing Jed and he's part of that. He's getting his work in, but we definitely like the idea that Jed is facing off with a good, veteran player."
   
 Stefanski acknowledged the importance of Bitonio's presence.
   
 "Joel is a pro. Very impressed by Joel on a personal level, as well. I think he's an outstanding teammate, and he has some pelts on the wall," he said. "He has played in a bunch of games, so I think that always helps when you are breaking in a rookie next to him at left tackle.
   
 "I think that Jed is very lucky to have a player of Joel's caliber but also just the type of person he is. I think that's going to be a really good relationship. I really think that group as whole, with [offensive line] coach [Bill] Callahan and how he runs that group, they have to play together. ... It's the old 'five of them make up a fist,' and Joel is a very big piece of that in making sure we have the right mentality for that group."
   
 Bitonio on Mayfield
   
 As a seven-year veteran, Bitonio's opinion carries significant weight among his peers. It's likely they will take note of what he's had to say about Mayfield, who recently confessed to having lost himself last season.
   
 Mayfield said he spoke with his teammates about that, pledging to do better, something Bitonio confirmed.
   
 "And then from a leadership perspective this year he has done everything right," Bitonio said. "He's trying to lead by example. Just looking at him when he showed up, he looked a lot better. His body looked better. He looked like he was moving really well. He looked strong. All the things you want to see in a quarterback."
   
 Bitonio said the body change and other things could have just been offseason talk.
   
 "But he really did take those steps. That is the first arc, lead by example, and then as a quarterback you automatically lead with your voice because you are the quarterback of this team," he said. "Just taking that step, you see him talking to some of the young receivers, or even Odell [Beckham Jr.] and Jarvis [Landry] and Nick [Chubb] and guys that have been around. We have a standard here now and that standard can change, and as the quarterback of this team, you are the standard. You are what people follow, and I think he understands that now. We are ready to help him push that standard."
   
 Under the lights
   
 Stefanski said getting in practices at FirstEnergy Stadium is a priority for the team, and he plans to do so later on in training camp.
   
 "We do want to get down there. We have two of them planned in the stadium later in camp. I think that is going to be important for us," he said. "We will put the players through a lot of game-like situations. That will be one of the ideas in getting to the stadium, getting used to the locker room and making it as much game-like as we can."

 The Browns waived undrafted rookie guard Jovahn Fair and signed center Casey Dunn on Sunday.

 Page first saw Sahl compete at the Hudson Junior and got another look at another big-time junior event. Less than one year later Sahl committed to KSU.
