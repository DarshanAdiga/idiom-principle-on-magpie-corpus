 # Salinas City Council District 1 candidates in neck and neck race
 Angelaydet Rocha
 Salinas Californian
 View Comments
 Salinas City Council District 1 last voting update show Carla Viviana González neck and neck with incumbent Scott Davis.
 Davis is in the lead with 2,556 votes (50.12%), but González isn't too far behind with 2,544 votes (49.88%).
 Davis started off strong Election Night leading with 1,685 (52.17%) votes and González trailing with 1,545 votes (47.83%). It didn’t take too long before González started catching up to Davis.
 By Monday, the two were separated by only .24%.
 Both candidates are long time members of the Salinas community. Making them both very familiar with the needs of the district they will oversee.
 González is an Alisal High school educator and co-founder of Salinas Valley Ethnic Studies Collaborative and the Monterey County Black & Brown Solidarity Coalition.
 She remains positive as the race results continue to unfold.
 "We’re still hopeful, definitely looking forward to hearing the next round of results. We’ve been receiving such encouraging messages from our supporters and thank them for their trust," she said. "Regardless of the results, we’re proud of the ideas we put forth and the policy platform we built."
 Davis did not respond to the request for comment.
