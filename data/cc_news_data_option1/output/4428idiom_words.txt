 # Lubbock reports 2 more COVID-linked deaths, 222 new cases
 Adam D. Young
 Lubbock Avalanche-Journal
 Lubbock confirmed two more people's death linked to COVID-19, along with 222 new coronavirus cases and 113 more recoveries on Saturday.
 The total number of confirmed cases is listed at 19,418 with 15,876 people recovered and 3,334 active cases, according to data provided by the City of Lubbock Health Department.
 Since March, 208 deaths have been linked to COVID-19.
 The Lubbock trauma service area remained above the 15% hospitalization threshold at 21.9% Saturday. Lubbock's trauma service area encompasses 22 counties around the South Plains and West Texas.
 Of the 264 people hospitalized with COVID-19 in Lubbock County as of Saturday, 57 were in an Intensive Care Unit. That's up five from the overall COVID-19 hospitalization number reported Friday.
 A total of 160,312 coronavirus tests have been processed in Lubbock, with a positive rating of 12.8 percent.
 As of Friday, Texas Tech reported there were 34 new active cases on campus, including 26 student case. Tech also saw 38 new recoveries, including 34 students. As of Friday, Tech is reporting 179 currently active cases, including 125 students.
 So far, Texas Tech has reported 2,201 total cases, with 1,896 of the cases being students and 305 being employees. Tech's numbers are included in Lubbock County's total, but may be off by a day.
 Across the state of Texas, there were 782,006 recovered, 102,769 confirmed active cases and 18,024 deaths linked to the coronavirus, according to data provided by the Department of State Health Services.
