 FRAMINGHAM -- Framingham has been a high-risk city for coronavirus spread for three weeks now, and community transmission is still on the rise, Framingham Health Director Sam Wong said Friday.
 Other municipalities tagged with the red-zone designation this week were Chatham, Chelsea, Dedham, Everett, Lawrence, Lynn, Lynnfield, Methuen, Monson, New Bedford, Revere and Winthrop.
 On Friday, Framingham announced that 27 residents had tested positive for the virus since Sept. 9, bringing the total number of active cases in the city to 97. Two more residents have died after contracting the virus, bringing Framingham's total death toll to 130.
