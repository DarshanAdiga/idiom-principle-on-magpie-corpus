 Blountstown (9-8) eliminated the Bucks (10-4) by scores of 25-12, 25-13, 33-31 to advance to Saturday's region final against Sneads.
 The finish wasn't as clean as the start for the Tigers, but head coach Leigh Ann Summers and her girls are happy just to be moving on.
 "It was a great fight," Summers said of the third set. "I ran out of substitutions there at the end and had a (defensive specialist) that had to go play outside for three rotations, but I'm proud of how they handled that. It was a nailbiter back and forth, but the kids stepped up and finally strung two points together.
 "We've got to be tougher on that, but I was proud of their effort and proud of the kids for having to step out of their comfort zone and play a position they've never practiced."
 For Bozeman, it's a quick end to its second-ever postseason appearance, but Bucks coach Sean Fields said the third set demonstrated the growth of his team over the past year.
 "You could see from the last set that the girls were capable of playing with Blountstown," he said. "They got almost kind of awestruck in the first set and had to kind of battle back some.  "But once we settled a little bit and got into our game I thought we competed well, and the girls never gave up. They played hard the whole time."

 The Tigers led from wire to wire in the second set, closing it out with a kill from Bailey and two straight serves from Cydnee Eubanks.
 In the third set, the Bucks took control with five straight points featuring four consecutive serves, including an ace, by Mackenzi Jones to go up 12-7. Blountstown rallied back to tie it up 16-16 on a kill by Kamryn Parrish, but Bozeman scored three of the next four points, getting a pair of kills from Kyra Pierce, to take a 20-17 advantage.
 Another kill from Hannah Flemming made it a 23-21 Bozeman lead, but Blountstown got two straight kills from Eubanks to tie it back up. An error by the Tigers gave the Bucks a set point, but a putaway by Parrish followed by a kill from Alyssa Carey turned the tables and gave Blountstown a match point at 25-24.
 A big block-kill by Pierce on the ensuing point kept the Bucks alive, as did three attacking errors and a service error on Blountstown's next four match points.
 A successful serve by Piper Messick ending with another Blountstown error gave Bozeman its second set point at 30-29, but Messick's next serve went out of bounds to tie it back up.
 After failing to convert on yet another match point two points later, the Tigers finally finished off the set and the match when a long rally ended with a Bozeman attacking error, with the ball hitting the floor just in front of the net in between a pair of Bucks players.
 Despite the loss and the end of the season, Fields said this year represented a major step forward for the program and is a sign of good things to come for a team that had just two seniors and one junior on the roster.
 "Absolutely, I think the girls, even in some of the games they won this year weren't pleased with how they played and they're not gonna be pleased with how they played tonight," Fields said. "This will sit bad with them for the whole offseason.  The small continent of South China on the Equator showed a difference of more than four metres in sea level between high and low tide. The existence of tides at the time has previously been verified through studies of geological strata, but determining the extent of the difference between low and high tide has not been feasible.
